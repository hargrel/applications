Agency Sample Application

This is a sample application to demonstrate how the interaction with the Queensland Government Client Identity Management System (QGCIDM) would work.

As a Queensland Government Agency, your website will make use of the QGCIDM in a similar way to this. That is, your website may have a number of different services on it with different security needs.

It will be up to you to determine which services require what level of protection.

Note the difference between a Credential Provider (CP) and an Identity (ID). CPs such as Google or AUSKey are used to log on to the system while the ID is the actual user data that is being protected.

As a matter of privacy, the QGCIDM stores very minimal information about an individual. These details are; first name, last name and date of birth. Other information, such as the user's email address may be requested by your application however delivery is on a best effort basis.

When a user verifies their identity documents online, the document details are discarded and the user is left with a tick or cross against the particular document that has been shown. Because sensitive user data is not kept, it cannot be lost or stolen.

  • Basic Use Case

    A basic CP with a basic ID.

    This combination provides basic assurance that a person is who they say they are, and is sufficient for most services that require a user to log in and provide minimal information about themselves.

  • Standard Use Case

    A secure CP with a verified ID.

    Used when the Agency service requires strong assurance that a person is who they say they are. Forces a user to log in securely and to have verified a minimal set of identity documents such as a Birth Certificate, Passport or Driver Licence.

  • High Security Use Case

    An extra secure CP with a true online ID.

    Not yet implemented. At present the technology required for this level of assurance is not available. Requires biometrics and/or proof of presence within the community as well as extra security measures during the log in process.
